Cap Studio — Cap Finder

Find Your Perfect Cap

Three questions. Hundreds of variables. One shortlist built around your brief, your decoration technique, and your customer's world.

01

Decoration technique

Embroidery, screen print, heat transfer, all-over or patches — each method suits specific cap constructions.

02

Silhouette & structure

Structured or unstructured, snapback or fitted, panel count — the shape drives everything from cost to decoration placement.

03

End use & customer

Sports, corporate, fashion, workwear, kids — the use case determines fabric weight, fit, and finish expectations.

Question 1 of 3

What decoration technique do you use?

Different methods require different fabric weights, panel counts, and crown structures. Selecting your technique first narrows the field significantly.

Embroidery
Thread-based stitching on structured panels
Screen Print
Ink pushed through mesh onto flat surfaces
Heat Transfer
Press-applied graphics, ideal for photographic detail
All-Over Print
Edge-to-edge coverage on uncut fabric panels
Patches & Labels
Woven, PVC or leather applied embellishments
Question 2 of 3

What silhouette do you need?

Crown structure affects decoration placement, embroidery stability, and the overall aesthetic your customer expects.

Structured cap
High front, firm crown — best for embroidery
Unstructured
Low profile, relaxed fit — soft crown aesthetic
Trucker
Structured front, mesh back, snapback closure
Bucket hat
Wide brim all around, unstructured crown
Beanie
Close-fitting knitwear with fold-over cuff
Neckwear
Scarves, snoods and gaiters
Question 3 of 3

What's the primary use?

End-use drives fabric weight, certification requirements, colour range priorities and expected price points.

Sports & Outdoors
Running, cycling, hiking, team sports
Corporate & Promotion
Events, gifting, branded uniform programmes
Fashion & Lifestyle
Streetwear, editorial, retail collections
Workwear
Trade, PPE programmes, site uniform
Kids & Youth
Schools, sports clubs, junior programmes
