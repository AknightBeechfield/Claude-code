DTG Print on Headwear

Digital printing for complex designs. No minimum quantities. Fast turnaround.

Print On Demand

DTG (Direct to Garment) printing applies water-based ink directly to the fabric surface using industrial inkjet technology. Think of it as a high-performance printer for textiles. It reproduces unlimited colours, photographic detail, gradients and fine text without screens, plates or setup charges — making it ideal for samples, short runs, personalised items and designs that would be prohibitively expensive to embroider.

Cotton and cotton-rich fabrics absorb DTG ink best, producing the sharpest, most vibrant results. Beechfield's organic cotton and EarthAware ranges are excellent candidates. For dark-coloured products, a white pre-treatment base coat is applied before printing so that colours remain vivid and opaque. Always specify fabric content when briefing your DTG supplier.

How DTG Printing Works

From artwork to finished product in three stages

1. Pre-Treatment

For dark or coloured fabrics, a liquid pre-treatment solution is sprayed onto the print area and heat-cured. This creates a bonding layer for the white ink base that sits beneath the colour inks. On white or very light fabrics, pre-treatment may be skipped, but it generally improves ink adhesion and wash durability on all colours. The product is then loaded onto a specialist cap platen that holds it flat under the print head.

Preparation Stage

2. Digital Printing

Your design file (high-resolution PNG, TIFF or PDF) is loaded into the printer's RIP software, which manages colour profiles, white ink coverage and print resolution. The printer lays down ink in multiple passes — white base layer first (on dark fabrics), then CMYK colour layers on top. Print resolution is typically 1200 x 1200 dpi, producing photographic-quality output. Each unit takes 30 seconds to 2 minutes depending on design complexity and coverage.

Production Stage

3. Curing

After printing, the ink must be heat-cured to set permanently into the fabric fibres. This is done using a heat press (typically 160-170C for 60-90 seconds) or a conveyor tunnel dryer for higher throughput. Proper curing is essential — under-cured prints will fade and wash out, while over-curing can scorch the fabric or yellow the white ink base. Once cured, DTG prints are rated for 50+ washes at 30C.

Finishing Stage

Technical Specifications

Typical DTG print areas for Beechfield headwear

Front Panel (Caps)

Maximum area: 100mm x 60mm. Cotton and cotton-blend caps give the sharpest results. Structured panels hold flat under the print head for even ink coverage.

Recommended

Side Panel (Caps)

Maximum area: 50mm x 40mm. Good for secondary branding. Some DTG cap platens allow rotation for side-panel printing without re-loading.

Good

Cuff (Beanies)

Maximum area: 80mm x 30mm. The doubled cuff provides a flat surface. Cotton beanies absorb ink well; acrylic beanies may require special ink formulations.

Good

Body (Morf)

Maximum area: 120mm x 80mm. Tubular knit Morfs offer a large, flat print area when stretched over a platen. Best with cotton-rich blends for ink absorption.

DTG Print FAQs

Can DTG print on polyester headwear?
Standard DTG inks are water-based and designed for cotton. Polyester fibres do not absorb water-based ink in the same way, which results in poor wash durability and dye migration (the polyester dye bleeds into the print). Some newer DTG systems offer polyester-compatible inks, but results vary. For polyester headwear, sublimation or heat transfer are generally better options. If DTG is required, always test on a sample first.
Is there a minimum order quantity for DTG?
No. One of DTG's biggest advantages is that there is no minimum order quantity. You can print a single unit economically — there are no screens, plates or setup charges. This makes DTG ideal for sampling, personalisation, short-run promotions and e-commerce fulfilment. However, per-unit cost is higher than screen print or transfers at volume, so for runs over 200-300 units, evaluate alternative methods.
How durable is DTG print after washing?
When properly pre-treated and cured, DTG prints are rated for 50+ washes at 30C. Wash inside-out on a gentle cycle and avoid tumble-drying at high heat. DTG prints have a soft hand feel because the ink absorbs into the fibre rather than sitting on top like a heat transfer. Over time, prints may soften slightly (similar to a vintage look) but should not crack, peel or flake like poorly applied transfers.
What resolution should my artwork be?
Supply artwork at a minimum of 300dpi at the actual print size. For headwear, the print area is small (typically 100mm x 60mm for cap fronts), so even a modest file will meet this threshold. PNG with transparent background is the preferred format. Vector files (AI, EPS) are also excellent. Avoid JPEG compression artefacts — they will print exactly as they appear on screen. Your DTG supplier can advise on colour profiles (typically sRGB or Adobe RGB).
How does DTG compare to heat transfer?
DTG prints directly onto the fabric, producing a softer hand feel with no visible film or edge. Heat transfers sit on top of the fabric as a thin film. DTG is better for single units and very small runs (no setup cost). Transfers are more versatile across different fabric types and generally more cost-effective at volumes above 50 units. DTG requires cotton-rich substrates; transfers work on cotton, polyester and blends. For headwear, the choice often depends on fabric content and order quantity.
