Proper Fit for Young Heads

Kids headwear is not just adult headwear made smaller. Beechfield's junior range is specifically sized and proportioned for children — with the same decoration performance, quality standards and colour consistency as the adult range.

Schools, youth clubs, holiday camps, children's parties — wherever kids need branded headwear, the junior range delivers. Matching styles available in adult sizes too, so the whole group can coordinate.

Why Beechfield for Kids Headwear?

Purpose-Sized for Children

Junior headwear requires reduced crown depth, shorter peak length and adjusted circumference to fit ages 3-12 comfortably. Beechfield junior styles are specifically patterned for children's proportions — not simply scaled-down adult products. The result is headwear that fits properly, looks right and stays on during play.

Matching Adult-Junior Styles

Schools and clubs often need the same branded cap or beanie in both adult and junior sizes — for staff and students, coaches and players, parents and children. Key Beechfield styles are available in matching adult and junior versions with identical colours, construction and decoration positioning. One design, one setup, two size ranges.

Safe, Tested Materials

Parents and schools rightly care about what touches children's skin. Key junior products carry OEKO-TEX Standard 100 certification, confirming they have been tested for harmful substances. Organic cotton options from the EarthAware range provide an additional reassurance for eco-conscious schools and parents.

What to Look For in Kids Headwear

Choosing headwear for children means balancing fit, safety, durability and decoration requirements specific to this age group.

Correct Sizing

Children's head circumference varies significantly between ages 3 and 12. Junior caps with adjustable closures (velcro or buckle) cover the broadest age range in a single SKU. For beanies, stretchy acrylic or cotton knits accommodate growth without the cap becoming too loose or too tight. Always verify junior sizing against the specific Beechfield size chart.

Durability for Active Wear

Children are hard on headwear. Caps get stuffed into bags, beanies get pulled and stretched, bucket hats get dragged through mud. Choose products with reinforced stitching and machine-washable fabrics. Acrylic beanies survive more wash cycles than cotton at this price point. Structured caps hold their shape better than unstructured options when subjected to backpack compression.

Bright, Appealing Colours

Kids respond to colour. While school briefs may dictate navy or bottle green, youth clubs, holiday programmes and children's events benefit from bright, fun colourways. Beechfield's junior range includes vibrant options — sky blue, red, pink, yellow — alongside the traditional school colours. Bright headwear also improves visibility on trips and outdoor activities.

Decoration Guide for Kids Headwear

Decoration on junior headwear requires scaled-down design sizes to suit the smaller product. A logo that works at 80mm wide on an adult cap may need reducing to 60mm for a junior version. Maintain the same thread colours and design proportions, but reduce the overall footprint to match the smaller panel area.

For school orders, embroidery is the preferred method — it survives the repeated washing that children's headwear inevitably requires. Keep designs simple with limited colour changes (4-6 thread colours maximum) to maintain quality at junior scale. Heat-transfer prints work well for multi-colour school crests that would be too complex for embroidery. Always run a junior-specific sample before committing to production.
