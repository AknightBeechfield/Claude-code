What to Look For in Sports Headwear

Choosing the right headwear for sport means understanding the demands of training, competition and sideline conditions.

Moisture-Wicking Construction

During high-intensity activity, sweat management is critical. Look for caps with technical sweatbands and lightweight polyester shells that move moisture away from the forehead. The B188 Technical Running Cap is purpose-built for this — a featherweight shell with full wicking capability.

Ventilation and Airflow

Mesh back panels and eyelets are not cosmetic in sports headwear — they are functional. Proper ventilation prevents heat build-up during sustained effort. Trucker-style caps with mesh backs offer maximum airflow for warm-weather sport, while structured front panels still deliver clean embroidery surfaces.

Sideline Warmth

Not all sports headwear is about keeping cool. Sideline beanies, dugout warmers and pre-match hats need thermal performance. Double-layer knit beanies with deep cuffs provide warmth for spectators, substitutes and winter training. Cuff embroidery keeps the team crest visible.

Decoration Guide for Sports Headwear

Sports headwear decoration needs to survive wash cycles, sweat exposure and physical contact. Embroidery is the default for team crests — it withstands laundering far better than print on performance fabrics. Position club badges on the front centre panel and keep stitch counts moderate to avoid puckering on lightweight shells.

For technical caps like the B188, heat-transfer is the best option — the ultra-light fabric is too fine for heavy embroidery. Beanies take cuff embroidery exceptionally well; use a flat-back design with bold thread weights for maximum visibility from the stands. For large event orders, screen print on cotton caps offers the best cost-per-unit at volume.
